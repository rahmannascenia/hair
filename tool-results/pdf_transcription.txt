1. Company Overview
Barindra International is a leading human hair processing and manufacturing company based in
Bangladesh. The company specializes in procuring raw human hair from various countries, processing
it through multiple stages, and exporting the final products to international markets.
The company operates a unique business model combining home-based workers with factory-based
processing to achieve scale and efficiency. With over 5,000 workers and a sophisticated supply chain,
Barindra International has established itself as Bangladesh's first formal exporter in the human hair
industry.

2. Raw Material Procurement Process
The procurement process is the foundation of the entire business operation. Raw human hair is
sourced both internationally and locally. The procurement team maintains relationships with
suppliers across multiple countries to ensure consistent supply of quality raw materials.

2.1 Import Procurement
Raw human hair is imported from multiple countries including India, Uzbekistan, Tajikistan, Pakistan,
Myanmar, China, Nepal, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The import process involves:
1. Supplier Selection & Negotiation: Identify and negotiate with suppliers in source countries. Price
varies significantly based on hair quality, length, and origin.
2. Letter of Credit (LC) Issuance: Commercial bank issues LC for international purchases. LC
management tracks from opening to clearance.
3. Shipping & Customs Clearance: Goods shipped to Bangladesh and cleared through customs at
ports.
4. Transport to Dhaka Warehouse: Cleared goods transported to main warehouse in Dhaka for
sorting and grading.

2.2 Local Procurement
In addition to imports, raw hair is also purchased locally from various regions within Bangladesh.
Local procurement involves direct purchase from collectors and intermediaries. Prices vary by
location (e.g., Narayanganj, Gazipur, and other areas have different rates for similar quality).
• Local suppliers bring raw hair to the Dhaka office
• Quality assessment performed on-site before purchase
• Immediate cash payment or credit arrangement
• Goods直接进入 sorting process without customs clearance

3. Raw Material Receiving & Inventory
All incoming raw materials (both imported and locally purchased) are received at the Dhaka
warehouse. This stage is critical as it establishes the foundation for all subsequent tracking and
costing.

Receiving Process Steps
1. Physical Receipt: Goods are physically received and counted at the warehouse.
2. Weight Recording: Initial weight is recorded (e.g., 500 kg per batch).
3. Quality Inspection: Initial quality check to verify it matches purchase order specifications.
4. Inventory Entry: Goods are entered into stock as 'Raw Material Stock' with purchase value.
5. Lot/Batch Number Assignment: A unique lot number is assigned for tracking purposes.

4. Sorting, Grading & Lot Assignment
The sorting and grading process is one of the most critical stages in the entire operation. Experienced
workers in Dhaka sort the raw hair by color and quality before assigning lot numbers. This process
requires significant expertise as the quality assessment directly impacts pricing and processing
decisions.

Sorting Process
1. Color Separation: Hair is separated by natural color categories (black, brown, blonde, etc.).
2. Quality Assessment: Each batch is assessed for texture, strength, length consistency, and overall
quality.

5. Material Transfer: Dhaka to Dinajpur
After sorting and lot assignment in Dhaka, materials are transferred to the Dinajpur factory for
processing. This transfer process must be carefully tracked to maintain inventory accuracy.

Transfer Process
1. Requisition: Production Manager or Group Head creates a requisition for materials needed at
Dinajpur.
2. Material Booking: Requested materials are booked against specific lot numbers in the system.
3. Dispatch from Dhaka: Materials physically dispatched with proper documentation (delivery slip).
4. Transport: Goods transported via road to Dinajpur factory.
5. Receipt at Dinajpur: Materials received and verified against requisition at factory.
6. Stock Update: Dhaka stock reduced, Dinajpur stock increased accordingly.

Tracking Points
• Booking status: booked vs. physically dispatched
• In-transit tracking
• Receipt confirmation and variance check

6. Washing & Pre-Processing
Upon receipt at the Dinajpur factory, raw hair undergoes the first major processing phase — washing
and pre-treatment. This stage transforms the raw, unprocessed hair into clean, sanitized material
ready for production.

Washing Process Steps
1. Initial Sorting: Received lots are organized by the floor manager.
2. Washing: Hair is washed using specialized shampoo and chemicals in the washing plant.
3. Chemical Treatment: Specific chemicals applied based on hair type and desired output.
4. Drying: Hair is dried using industrial drying equipment.
5. Re-stocking: Cleaned hair is weighed and restocked as 'Washed Material' (weight may vary ±2kg).

Personnel Involved

Oversees washing operations

Washing Plant Workers

Checks wash quality before handover to production

7.2 Material Distribution Flow
1. Production Manager (PM): Receives washed material and plans production distribution. Assigns
targets to Group Heads.
2. Group Head (Head Leader): Receives 200-300 kg allocation. Divides among 8-10 Line Leaders.
Records lot numbers and quantities distributed.
3. Line Leader (Field Supervisor): Receives 30 kg allocation. Travels by motorcycle to factory areas.
Distributes to 10 Supervisors (each getting 3 kg).
4. Supervisor (Home-Based): Receives material for 15-20 workers. Organizes workers at her home or
nearby location. Distributes 100-300g per worker based on capacity.
5. Worker (Home-Based): Performs production tasks (weft making, alignment, etc.) at supervisor's
location or nearby homes.

Daily Production Cycle
• Morning: Line Leaders collect material from Group Head
• Day: Workers process material at their locations
• Evening: Workers return completed work to Supervisor
• Evening: Supervisor returns all packets to Line Leader
• Evening: Line Leader delivers to Group Head with worker reports

8. Quality Control & Grading (A/B/C)
Quality control is the cornerstone of Barindra International's business model. Unlike many
competitors, the company prioritizes quality over quantity. A worker producing 100g of best quality
earns more than one producing 5kg of poor quality.

QC Process
1. Packet Collection: All packets from a Line Leader's workers are collected and brought to the QC
station.

2. Individual Inspection: 4 QC inspectors examine each worker's output individually.
3. Quality Grading: Each packet is graded as A, B, or C based on:
• Length consistency
• Hair strength and texture
• Color uniformity
• Tangle-free quality
• Overall finish

Salary Impact
Worker salary is directly linked to quality grade:
• Grade A workers receive premium daily rate
• Grade B workers receive standard daily rate
• Grade C workers receive reduced daily rate
• Continuous Grade C performance may lead to termination
• Performance bonus calculated based on quality consistency

10. Phase 2 Production: Factory Processing
After Phase 1 completion, materials return to the Dinajpur factory for the second major processing
stage. Approximately 200 workers are employed at the factory for this phase.

Phase 2 Process
1. Material Receipt: Phase 1 output received from Group Heads as individual packets by Line Leader.
2. Consolidation: Packets consolidated by lot number.
3. Second Processing: 200 factory workers perform additional processing steps:
• Further alignment and sorting
• Length-based categorization
• Removal of imperfect strands
• Intermediate quality checks
4. Wastage Recording: Additional weight loss recorded at this stage.
5. Re-packing: Material repacked by quality and size for final processing.
6. Dispatch to Dhaka: Completed Phase 2 material sent back to Dhaka factory.

11. Final Processing & Size Sorting
The final processing stage takes place at the Dhaka factory where approximately 100 workers
complete the last processing steps before the products are ready for export.

Final Processing Steps
1. Receipt from Dinajpur: Phase 2 output received at Dhaka.
2. Final Quality Control: Final QC inspection by dedicated Quality Controller.
3. Size Sorting: Hair sorted by length in inches:
• Sizes range from 5 inches to 30 inches
• Each size category sorted separately
• Size availability tracked for order fulfillment
4. Final Trimming: Last trimming and finishing.
5. Export Preparation: Products packaged and labeled for export.

13. LC Management & Import Documentation
Letter of Credit management is critical for international procurement. The company handles multiple
LCs simultaneously across different countries and banks.

LC Management Process
1. LC Opening: LC opened through commercial bank for supplier.
2. Amendments: Any amendments tracked and documented.
3. Shipment Tracking: Monitor shipment against LC timeline.
4. Document Receipt: Receive and verify documents from negotiating bank.
5. Payment Settlement: LC payment settlement upon document verification.
6. LC Closure: Formal closure after all obligations met.

Key Tracking Requirements
• LC status: Open, Utilized, Closed
• Shipment status per LC
• Document status: Received, Pending, Discrepant
• Bank communication log

14. Worker Attendance & Salary Management
Worker management is one of the most complex aspects of operations. With approximately 5,000
daily workers, the attendance and salary calculation process requires careful tracking and multiple
verification steps.

Attendance Tracking
1. Daily Attendance: Each worker's attendance marked daily by their Supervisor.
2. Supervisor Report: Supervisor submits attendance report to Line Leader.
3. Line Leader Verification: Line Leader verifies and consolidates attendance data.
4. Data Entry: Two dedicated staff enter 5,000 workers' daily data.
5. Attendance Bonus: Workers with 30 consecutive days attendance receive bonus.

Salary Calculation
Component

Description

Bonus Types
• Attendance Bonus: For 30-day perfect attendance
• Performance Bonus: Based on supervisor's profit/loss contribution
• Supervisors generating profit receive production bonus
• Supervisors generating consistent loss may be terminated
